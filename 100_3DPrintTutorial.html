3D Print tutorial

From Digital Design to physical model

Category: Fabrication, Mechanical  |  Level: 100 - Introductory

Got 3D design ? Before you can print, you need to turn your 3D model into instructions the machine understands. This guide walks you through using PrusaSlicer to prepare your first print.

Prerequisites

  • Install PrusaSlicer Software on your computer.
  • A 3D Model File (STL, STEP, 3MF, or OBJ).

Part 1: The Software (PrusaSlicer)

Step 1: Importing & Orientation

  1. Open PrusaSlicer.
  2. Drag and Drop your STL/3MF file into the window.
  3. Orient the Model: Ideally, the largest flat surface should be on the bed.
    • Press F (Place on Face) and click a white plane on your model to snap it flat.
    • Press R to rotate manually if needed.

Step 2: Settings & Slicing

Check the Right Panel settings:

  • Printer: Select "Original Prusa i3 MK3S & MK3S+".
  • Filament: "Prusament PLA" (or Generic PLA).
  • Print Settings: "0.20mm QUALITY" is the best starting point.
  • Infill: Set to 20%.
  • Supports: Select "None" unless your model has steep overhangs.

Click Slice Now in the bottom right corner. Check the preview, then click Export G-code to save it to your SD Card.

Part 2: The Hardware (Printing)

Step 3: Preparing the Print Bed

The most common cause of print failure is a dirty surface. Fingerprints contain grease which prevents plastic from sticking.

  1. Ensure the magnetic steel sheet is on the bed.
  2. Pour a small amount of Isopropyl Alcohol (IPA) onto a paper towel.
  3. Wipe the bed thoroughly while it is cold.
Warning: Never clean the Textured PEI Powder-coated sheet with Acetone. Use only IPA (Alcohol).

Step 4: Loading Filament

  1. Press the control knob to open the Menu.
  2. Select Preheat → PLA. The printer will heat up (approx 215°C / 60°C).
  3. Wait for the temperature to stabilize.
  4. Take your filament roll and cut the tip at a 45-degree angle (sharp tip).
  5. Place the roll on the top holder.
  6. Insert the tip into the top of the extruder (the hole with the PTFE tube).
  7. Go to Menu → Load Filament (or Autoload will trigger).
  8. The printer will ask: "Filament extruding with correct color?" If you see plastic coming out of the nozzle, select YES.

Step 5: Starting the Print

  1. Insert the SD card containing your G-code.
  2. Press the knob and select Print from SD.
  3. Scroll to find your file (e.g., my_model_0.2mm_PLA_MK3S_2h.gcode).
  4. Press the knob to confirm.

The printer will perform "Mesh Bed Leveling" (probing 9 points on the bed) and then start printing.

Step 6: The First Layer (Critical!)

Do not walk away! Watch the first layer. The nozzle will draw a "skirt" (outline) and then the object.

Live Adjust Z: If the plastic is not sticking or looks like loose spaghetti, long-press the knob to enter Live Adjust Z.
  • Turn Counter-Clockwise (Negative): Lowers the nozzle (More squish).
  • Turn Clockwise (Positive): Raises the nozzle.
Goal: The line should be slightly flattened, not round.

Step 7: Removing the Print

Once the print is finished:

  1. Wait! Let the bed cool down below 40°C.
  2. Remove the magnetic steel sheet from the printer.
  3. Flex the sheet inward with both hands. The print should pop off effortlessly.
Caution: Never use a metal spatula or knife on the PEI sheet. You will scratch the coating permanently.

Wrapping Up

Congratulations on your first print! Remember to always keep your print sheet clean and store your filament in a dry place when not in use.
